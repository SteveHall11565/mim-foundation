01 · Positioning

A serious room for leaders who already understand the stakes.

Make It Matter Foundation does not exist to lecture business leaders, soften capitalism, or turn stewardship into a public campaign. It exists to convene leaders with the platform, judgment, and responsibility to shape what comes next.

The work is practical: bring the best intelligence to the leaders who can use it, convene them in rooms where commitments carry weight, and support the development that makes stewardship durable under pressure.

“Stewardship is not an obstacle to performance. It is the operating framework that protects it.”

The Case

Stewardship is a performance discipline.

It is not a values page, a public relations posture, or a compliance exercise. It is the way serious leaders protect the conditions that allow enterprise to flourish.

01 · Why now

The pressure on leaders is changing.

CEOs and boards are making decisions faster, with more complexity, under more scrutiny, and against a backdrop of accelerating technology. AI can expand opportunity at scale. It can also concentrate gains at a speed no previous generation of leaders has faced.

Make It Matter helps leaders navigate that pressure with stewardship as a governing principle: how to deploy powerful resources in ways that expand opportunity, strengthen trust, and protect long-term performance.

02 · Four areas of focus

Where stewardship is becoming operational.

AI

AI integration

Deploying AI to expand capability and opportunity, not simply to concentrate gains or reduce headcount.

Work

Workforce development

Building pipelines, reskilling, and internal mobility so people can participate in the next economy.

Supply

Resilient supply chains

Designing for breadth and resilience, not only lowest cost, in a world of geopolitical disruption.

Next

Emerging technology

Maintaining stewardship as a governing principle as quantum, crypto, AI, and new technologies accelerate decisions.

When more people have the opportunity to participate productively in capitalism, prosperity becomes broader, trust becomes stronger, and shareholder value becomes more durable.
Economic belief
03 · What this is not

No charity framing. No ideological posture. No lectures.

The foundation speaks to leaders as peers. It does not ask them to choose between performance and responsibility. It helps them see that durable performance depends on the health of the system, people, communities, and trust that make enterprise possible.

Not

CSR, ESG, virtue language, broad public activism, political signaling, or redistribution messaging.

Yes

Merit-based opportunity, voluntary stewardship, operating discipline, talent strength, resilience, and long-term value.

Proof

Use evidence selectively: performance, retention, resilience, social license, and risk reduction.

Tone

Calm, direct, warm, commercially fluent, and serious enough for rooms where decisions are made.

Stewardship protects the future of enterprise.

Leaders who practice stewardship are not stepping away from capitalism. They are strengthening the conditions that allow capitalism to remain trusted, productive, innovative, and free.

01 · The shift

The original conviction still matters. The leverage has moved upstream.

Make It Matter began with a conviction: not every philanthropic dollar has the same return, and one of the highest-return investments in the nonprofit sector is in the leader fighting the cause.

That work remains honorable. But the causes Steve cares about most — economic opportunity, workforce development, community resilience — are downstream of something larger: whether enterprise creates enough opportunity, trust, resilience, and shared prosperity to keep capitalism healthy.

The foundation is now focused on leaders whose companies, capital, platforms, and decisions can influence that upstream system.

A note from Steve Hall

For most of my life, the measure of a good year was what I had built. Success has a quiet way of changing that measure — from what I can create to what I have been given to look after.

I have spent much of my life learning that conscious leadership and strong performance are not in conflict. At their best, they are the same pursuit.

Make It Matter Foundation is my attempt to help more leaders practice that truth inside the institutions they already lead.

Steve Hall · Founder, Make It Matter Foundation
